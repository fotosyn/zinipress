Guide and Privacy

1. Add your images

Drop a batch onto the page, click Add images, or drag files onto individual panels. Name files so they auto-place:

  • imageC — front cover
  • image1 … image12 — interior pages
  • imageBC — back cover (fills both back cover panels)

Spaces, underscores, and hyphens are ignored; the extension does not matter. The Flap panel is instruction-only and cannot take an image. Files that do not match a panel name appear under Unused images — drag them onto the sheet manually. Legacy names imageX2 and imageX3 still work.

2. Arrange and edit

  • Drag between panels on the sheet or reading preview to move or swap images.
  • Drag inside an image on the sheet or reading preview to reposition it within its frame.
  • Click × on a panel to remove its image (it returns to Unused if it was not a mirror copy).
  • Hover the scissors beside the sheet to see where to cut (three panels across; one hinge panel stays attached).

3. Cover options

  • Spine — band on the back cover (flap); shifts the image so it aligns when glued to the back cover.
  • Tail — vertical credit strip on the back cover panel.
  • Solid back cover — flat colour and optional text on both back cover panels instead of photos.

4. Export and print

Click Download sheet for a print-ready PNG. Print at 100% scale on A4.

The reading preview below the sheet shows pages in order: Front cover → 1 → 2 → … → 12 → Back cover. You can drop and reposition images there too — changes stay in sync with the print sheet.

5. Folding and assembly

After printing, follow the Folding and assembly  (opens in new window) guide for creases, cuts, and how to put the zine together. Keep it open in a separate window while you work.

6. Privacy

Ziner runs entirely in your browser. There is no backend, no sign-in, and no database.

  • Your images stay on your device. Files you add are held in memory while you work. Nothing is uploaded to a server, stored by us, or shared with third parties.
  • Export is local. When you download the sheet, your browser saves the PNG to your device like any other file download.
  • Session only. Closing the tab or clicking Clear all removes images from this session. Refreshing the page also clears your work.
  • No tracking. This app does not use cookies, accounts, or analytics. It does not load third-party scripts or fonts.

You are responsible for the content of images you add (e.g. photos of other people). We cannot access them because they never leave your browser.
